Seminar in Statistics

  • Subjects : The concept of realized volatility as an alternative mechanism to model financial volatility: BTC market
  • University: Kiel University
  • Lecturer: Dr. Cristina Sattarhoff
  • Grade: 1.3 (A-)
  • Important references :
    1. Patton, A. (2011). “Data-based ranking of realised volatility estimators,” Journal of Econometrics, 161(2), 284–303.
    2. Soylu, P. K., Okur, M., Çatıkkaş, Ö., Altintig, Z. A. (2020). “Long memory in the volatility of selected cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin, Ethereum and Ripple,” Journal of risk and financial management, Vol. 13.2020, 6/107, 1-20
  • summary: It examines various realized measures to model financial volatility, specifically in the Bitcoin market. The paper explores different time periods, comparing pre-pandemic and pandemic periods, and analyzes the performance of these measures in the cryptocurrency market. It includes an in-depth analysis of data, methodologies for calculating realized measures, comparisons across different periods, and forecasting market volatility using the HAR model. The paper concludes with findings on the effectiveness of these measures in the Bitcoin market during different periods.
